Overall Objective

Strategize and design a conversational support platform that empowers experts to create chatbots, convert complex processes into Q&A-based workflows and maintain knowledge bases, reducing dependency on human support by 15% and $2.3M in projected savings.

VMware Genie is an AI-powered chatbot that was created to support VMware's digital transformation and provide timely support to employees. I was given the opportunity to play the dual-role of Product Owner and UX Designer for transforming the admin experience.

Goals for MVP

The existing admin portal was created by engineers and is not intuitive for new admins to use. Upcoming changes to VMware's strategy requires Genie to take on an important, customer-facing role. To serve our customers efficiently, we had to enable to admins to manage their domain data.

Simplify the interface for managing large Knowledge Bases with thousands of questions.
Enable domain experts to create, deploy and manage chatbots on their own.
Facilitate conversion of complex processes into Q&A-based workflows.
Provide detailed analytics & feedback data for Admins to improve their conversation flows.
My role as the Product Owner & Lead Designer
This case study includes only high-level details. I'm unable to share specifics publicly due to confidentiality. Please get in touch if you'd like to learn more about the product or my process.

Define vision & strategy, get buy-in from leadership

I defined the vision, short-term and long-term strategies to bring the different sides of Genie together coherently.

This was presented to senior leadership within VMware IT to get their approval and resources allocated.

Conduct user research to finalize requirements and roadmap

I conducted multiple interviews and organized 2 workshops that brought together admins from different domains to capture their requirements for the new admin portal.

The requested features were then prioritized and the most impactful features were identified for the MVP.

Document the new information architecture, user roles & permissions

To make the new features of the admin portal intutive to navigate, I designed the new information architecture. This involved grouping related channels into top-level domains, which could contain multiple knowledge bases, chatbots and conversational flows.

Based on user input, I also defined the 3 user roles and their permissions: Domain Admin (full access), Contributor (limited access) and Stakeholder (limited read-only access).

Create a new design system

Using the Atomic Design principles, I created a new component library that builds on top of VMware's Clarity design system.

More than 20 new components were created leveraging Figma's Auto Layout and Variant capabilities. This made creating mockups as simple as dragging-and-dropping components from the library.

Design mockups and interactive prototypes

Using the newly created design system, I created more than 36 pixel-perfect mockups for the knowledge base, conversation flow, domain management and user management features.

The mockups also include call-outs that guide developers during implementation. Please refer to the next section below to see some full-sized mockups.

Conduct usability tests with admins

I recruited 3 admins for usability testing. They were presented with different scenarios such as creating a new question in the knowledge base, adding region-specific answers, managing incoming questions etc.

The RITE method of usability testing was followed wherein issues are categorized into 3 buckets: obvious issues with quick fixes, obvious issues that need more time to fix and issues without an obvious cause.

No major issues were found and all minor ones were fixed immediately.

Hand-off mockups & flows to developers

After the mockups for the MVP features were finalized, I organized a kick-off session with developers where I walked them through the different flows and features in the new portal.

I am supporting the developers by providing HTML templates and production-ready CSS whenever necessary.
