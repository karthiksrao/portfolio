About Genie

Genie is an employee support chatbot built to support VMware's digital transformation by instantly answering employee questions on technology, people, and administrative processes. It became indispensible during the shift to remote work.

Genie is powered by knowledge bases that are maintained by internal domain experts. Teams across the organization such as HR, IT, Finance and Operations etc have dedicated experts and agents to manage their domain's knowledge-bases and converse with employees via live chat.

Upcoming changes to VMware's strategy requires Genie to take on an important, customer-facing role. To serve our customers efficiently, we had to onboard and empower more admins to manage their domain's knowledge-base.

In the absence of a designated product manager, I played the dual role of Product Manager and UX Designer for the redesign of Genie's knowledge-base management application.

PM

Defined vision & strategy, got buy-in from leadership

Before starting the design process, we needed clarity on Genie's future. I defined the vision, short-term and long-term strategies to bring the different sides of Genie together coherently. This was presented to senior leadership within VMware IT to get their approval and resources allocated.

UX

Conducted user research to understand user needs & challenges

I completed a heuristic analysis of the existing knowledge-base management application to understand it's limitations and challenges. I found major usability issues, such as unintuitive navigation and confusing workflows and terminology.

The existing knowledge-base management application had several usability issues.

I then conducted user interviews and contextual inquiries with admins from different domains to capture their experience of using the admin portal. The existing admin portal was created by engineers and is not intuitive for new admins to use.

Based on user feedback, it became evident the existing application did not accurately represent the user's mental-model of a knowledge-base and required a complete redesign.

PM

Facilitated workshops to capture user requirements

I facilitated 2 workshops that brought together admins from different domains to capture their requirements for the new admin portal.

I then collaborated with the engineering team to prioritize the requested features and create a roadmap. We set the following goals for the MVP:

Simplify the interface for managing large Knowledge Bases with thousands of questions.
Enable domain experts to create, deploy and manage chatbots on their own.
Facilitate conversion of complex processes into Q&A-based workflows.
Provide detailed analytics & feedback data for Admins to improve their conversation flows.
UX

Documented the new information architecture, user roles & permissions

To make the new features of the admin portal intutive to navigate, I designed the new information architecture. This involved grouping related channels into top-level domains, which could contain multiple knowledge bases, chatbots and conversational flows.

Based on user input, I also defined the 3 user roles and their permissions:

  • Domain Admin (full access)
  • Contributor (limited access)
  • Stakeholder (limited, read-only access)

UX

Created a new design system

Using the Atomic Design principles, I created a new component library that builds on top of the VMware Clarity design system.

More than 20 new components were created leveraging Figma's Auto Layout and Variant capabilities. This made creating mockups as simple as dragging-and-dropping components from the library.

UX

Conducted usability tests with end users

I recruited 3 admins for usability testing. They were presented with different scenarios such as creating a new question in the knowledge base, adding region-specific answers, managing incoming questions etc.

The RITE method of usability testing was followed wherein issues are categorized into 3 buckets: obvious issues with quick fixes, obvious issues that need more time to fix and issues without an obvious cause.

No major issues were found and all minor ones were fixed immediately.

Handed-off mockups & flows to developers

After the mockups for the MVP features were finalized, I facilitated a kick-off session with developers where I walked them through the different flows and features in the new portal. I am supporting the developers by providing HTML templates and production-ready CSS whenever necessary.

The mockups also include call-outs that guide developers during implementation.

PM

To increase adoption, demonstrated capabilities to internal teams

The Professional Services team within VMware had estimated they could save around $2.3m in operating expenses if they automate some of their processes using Genie.

To raise awareness of Genie and find more opportunities for OpEx savings, I used the interactive prototypes to showcase Genie's capabilities to other teams. As of February 2023, we have 2 more teams who are ready to leverage Genie.
